Different Country Flags

Indian Flag :

Three equal horizontal stripes: saffron (top), white (middle), and green (bottom), symbolizing courage, peace, and prosperity respectively Ashoka Chakra: a navy-blue 24‑spoke wheel sits in the white stripe’s center, representing the eternal law of dharma and continuous progress Official since 1947: adopted by India’s Constituent Assembly on July 22 1947 and hoisted for the first time at independence (August 15); designed by Pingali Venkayya


Moldova Flag :

The flag of Moldova features a vertical tricolor of blue, yellow, and red (1:2 ratio), echoing its cultural and historical connection to Romania . Centered on the yellow stripe is the national coat of arms: a golden eagle holding a cross in its beak, a scepter in one talon and an olive branch in the other—symbols of faith, sovereignty, and peace . On the eagle’s chest is a shield displaying the traditional aurochs’ head, flanked by a rose and crescent with a star above—a nod to Moldova’s medieval heritage . Adopted on April 27, 1990, and formally reaffirmed by law in 2010, the flag represents Moldova’s rebirth as an independent democratic nation rooted in centuries-old traditions

United States

The flag of the United States, often called the Stars and Stripes, consists of 13 horizontal red and white stripes representing the original 13 colonies, and a blue canton adorned with white stars—one for each of the 50 states 🌟. The first official design was adopted on June 14, 1777 (now observed as Flag Day), directing “thirteen stripes, alternate red and white; that the union be thirteen stars” . As the nation grew, the number of stars increased, but the stripes were fixed at 13 in 1818 to honor the founding states .President Taft standardized its proportions in 1912, and precise colors were defined in 1934 .
